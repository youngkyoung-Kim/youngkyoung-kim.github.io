Research Overview

My research focuses on ferroelectric field-effect transistors (FeFETs) and oxide semiconductor devices for non-volatile memory and neuromorphic computing applications. I am particularly interested in device physics, interface engineering, and reliability characteristics such as retention and endurance.

Major Topics

  • HfO₂-based ferroelectric thin films (HZO, HSO) and phase stability
  • MFS / MIFS FeFET structures and memory window engineering
  • Oxide semiconductor channels (IGZO, ITZO) in FeFET and TFT
  • Charge trapping / detrapping and interface defect analysis
  • Retention, endurance, and variability in emerging memories
  • Synaptic behavior and multi-level programming for neuromorphic systems

Current Projects

1. MIFS-Structured IGZO FeFET

Investigating the impact of gate work function and interlayer (IL) design on memory window, retention, and endurance. The project includes fabrication of MFS and MIFS FeFETs, P–V and ID–VG characterization, and pulse-based program/erase analysis.

2. Oxide Semiconductor TFTs for Neuromorphic Devices

Developing a-IGZO / a-ITZO based TFT and FeTFT platforms with stable multi-level conductance states. The goal is to emulate synaptic behavior such as long-term potentiation (LTP), long-term depression (LTD), and paired-pulse facilitation (PPF).

3. Retention and Endurance Reliability

Systematic study of retention loss and endurance degradation mechanisms in FeFETs. This includes time-dependent read-out, pulse stress tests, and modeling of trap-assisted polarization switching.
